# Call 911? Decision Trainer

Knowing *when* a seizure becomes a 911 emergency is one of the most useful things a staff member
or family member can learn. Read each scenario and decide: call 911, or stay and monitor? You'll
get feedback each time.

## The rule it reinforces
**Call 911 if:**
- A convulsive seizure lasts **longer than 5 minutes**
- Seizures **repeat** without the person recovering in between
- It happens **in water**
- There's a **serious injury** or **trouble breathing** afterward
- It's a **first-ever** seizure
- The student's **seizure action plan** says to

For a typical seizure that stops on its own in a known student, you stay, keep them safe, time it,
and monitor. **When in doubt, call.** See [Seizure First Aid](../../chapters/02-seizure-first-aid/index.md).

!!! warning
    A learning tool, not medical advice. In a real emergency, always call 911.
