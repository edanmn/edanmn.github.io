## Why direct outreach beats a wire for this story

Paid and free press-release *wires* mostly buy a hosted URL and SEO, not real
coverage. For a Minnesota public-good story with a genuine data finding (the
~70% gap) and a compelling person (a student founder), **emailing local
reporters directly and handing the release to topical advocacy networks will
out-perform any wire — and it's free.** Treat wires/free PR sites as a
supplementary SEO move, not the main play.

## A) The pitch email (cover note)

Personalize the first line for each reporter. Keep it short — reporters skim.

**Subject-line options** (lead with the data):

1. `MN data: 7 in 10 school districts post no seizure plan — despite a state law`
2. `Student-built tool maps which Minnesota districts lack a seizure plan`
3. `Story idea: the school-safety law most MN districts haven't visibly met`

**Template:**

```text
Subject: MN data: 7 in 10 school districts post no seizure plan — despite a state law

Hi [First name],

[One sentence of personalization — reference a recent story they wrote on schools,
student health, or special education.] I have a Minnesota data story that fits your
[education / health] beat.

Since the 2022–23 school year, state law (§ 121A.24) has required every district to be
ready for student seizures. But a new analysis by a student-led group — the Epilepsy
Data & Advocacy Network (EDAN) — found that 231 of Minnesota's 328 districts (about 70%)
post no publicly findable seizure plan, and roughly half have no licensed school nurse.
The gap is concentrated in Greater Minnesota and tracks district size, not rural location.

Instead of just flagging noncompliance, the group built a free, plain-language textbook
and a district-lookup tool to help schools close the gap: https://edanmn.github.io/

The founder, Rishik Kondadadi — a Minnesota high school student — is available for an
interview, and I can share the district-by-district data behind the analysis.

Full release is below and attached as a PDF. Happy to connect you with Rishik.

[Your name]
[Phone] · [email]
```

**Beat tweaks** (swap the angle, keep the body):

- **Health desk:** subject → *"Most school seizures are handled by teachers, not nurses — and half of MN districts have none."*
- **Greater-MN / regional papers:** subject → *"How [their county/region]'s districts rank on seizure readiness"*; point them at the lookup tool for the local angle.
- **TV:** emphasize the **visual + person** — Rishik demoing the tool, plus a school nurse or parent on camera.

**Sending rules that matter:**

- One reporter at a time — **never a BCC blast**.
- Paste the release in the body **and** attach the PDF.
- Send **one** polite follow-up after 3–4 days if there's no reply.

## How to find the actual reporter (don't guess an address)

- Google `[outlet] education reporter`, or find the byline on a recent
  school-nurse / IEP / student-health story and email **that** person.
- Most MN outlets use `first.last@outlet.com` and have a staff directory or a
  **news-tip form / `tips@`** address.
- **Muck Rack** or LinkedIn confirm current beat assignments.

*Reporter names and emails are intentionally omitted here — verify the current
beat contact before sending, since assignments change often.*
